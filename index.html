Situation

Cement plants, energy facilities, and process-industry sites generate enormous volumes of sensor data — temperature, vibration, pressure, energy consumption — yet operators relied on periodic manual inspections and reactive maintenance cycles. Equipment failures were causing unplanned downtime with no unified intelligence layer to surface predictive signals from the noise.

Task

As the AI & IIoT Engineer Intern, I owned the full stack — from PLC and sensor connectivity at the factory floor to AI-driven analytics and an operator-facing copilot — with the mandate to make it production-ready for real industrial clients, not just a proof of concept.

Action

Built real-time data acquisition pipelines connecting PLCs, sensors, and edge devices to centralized analytics. Developed n8n automation workflows for ingestion, alerting, and AI inference routing. Architected MCP + RAG pipelines — grounding LLM agents in proprietary maintenance manuals and historical fault logs — to create an Industrial Copilot operators query in natural language. Monitoring dashboards tied together equipment health KPIs, fault detection, and predictive maintenance recommendations in a single view.

Result

Delivered a factory-floor-to-cloud intelligence platform deployed across 3 industrial client sites in cement and energy sectors.
